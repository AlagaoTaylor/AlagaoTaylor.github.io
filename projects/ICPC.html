── ICPC Coding Competitions ──

Details

  • Name: ICPC North America Pacific Northwest Regional Contest
  • Date: November 2024
  • Located: University of Hawaii at Hilo
  • Result: Valuable learning experience, while proudly representing Hawaii Pacific University.

    • International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) Regional Contest is a high-level university programming competition, where teams solve as many 8-12 algorithmic programming problems in 5 hours, as they can. Each team has 3 members and each team represents their university. Teams are ranked by a combination of factors that include: how many correct solutions submitted, the number of incorrect submissions, and how long it took to solve the programming problem.

Skills Developed

  • Problem-solving under time pressure
  • Debugging and optimizing code efficiently
  • Understanding algorithms and data structures
  • Time management in a competitive environment
  • Teamwork and collaboration

Reflections

Although my team and I did not place highly, the experience taught me the importance of preparation, staying calm under pressure, and continuous learning. At the end of the several hour time period and discovering the results, I didn't feel defeated. I knew that my teammates, peers, and fellow contestants all worked hard to solve tough problems and that I we all should be proud to represent our school at a regional level.

The semester was spent in preparation for this contest by maving smaller contests on Kattis, going against classmates 1-on-1, and collaborating when no one could find a great solution. Competitions are not only about ranking but also about growth and experience. A CS elective class turned into this great opportunity and besides the contest itself, I had a blast traveling to the Big Island with classmates and making memories I'll never forget.

This experience also motivated me to improve my coding skills (Python and Java) and tackle more challenging problems in the future.
